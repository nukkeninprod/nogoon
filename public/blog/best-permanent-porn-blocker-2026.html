The fundamental problem with every blocker app

Every single app-based blocker — Cold Turkey, Covenant Eyes, Qustodio, BlockerX, Canopy — shares the same fatal flaw: they're apps.

Apps can be:

  • Uninstalled (yes, even with "uninstall protection")
  • Disabled in System Settings or Task Manager
  • Bypassed with a different browser or profile
  • Circumvented by booting into safe mode
  • Removed by creating a new user account

When the urge hits at 2AM, your rational brain goes offline. You will find a way to delete the app. I know because I did. Many times. The "uninstall protection" they all advertise? It buys you maybe 5 minutes of friction. Not enough.

Quick breakdown of each

Cold Turkey ($39/yr or $99 lifetime)

The most popular option. Good general-purpose blocker for websites and apps. It has a "locked block" mode that makes it hard (not impossible) to reverse. But at the end of the day it's a macOS/Windows app sitting in your Applications folder. Apps can be removed. The porn-specific blocklists are decent but not comprehensive.

Covenant Eyes ($16.99/mo — $204/year)

The big one in religious communities. But here's what most people don't realize: Covenant Eyes doesn't block porn at all. It monitors your screen with AI and sends reports to an "accountability partner." You can still access everything — it just tells on you afterward. Plus it takes screenshots of your entire screen, including personal emails, bank dashboards, and private messages. That's $204/year for a privacy nightmare that doesn't actually prevent anything.

Qustodio ($54.95/yr)

A parental control tool that some adults use for self-blocking. Overkill for this use case — you're paying for screen time limits, location tracking, and app monitoring you don't need. And like all apps, it can be uninstalled with admin access (which you have, because it's your computer).

BlockerX / Canopy ($7.99/mo each)

Mobile-first blockers with desktop versions bolted on. Subscription model means you're paying ~$96/year indefinitely. The desktop blocking is weak compared to their mobile apps. Both can be uninstalled.

Hosts file (free, manual)

The free DIY approach: you open /etc/hosts on Mac or C:\Windows\System32\drivers\etc\hosts on Windows and manually add porn domains pointed to 0.0.0.0. Problem: there are thousands of domains to add, you'll miss most of them, and you can edit the file back in 10 seconds. Zero friction.
